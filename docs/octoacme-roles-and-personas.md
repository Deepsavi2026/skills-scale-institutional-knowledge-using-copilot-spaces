# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## QA / Testing Engineers

### Role Summary
QA/Testing Engineers validate that delivered software meets acceptance criteria, quality standards, and user expectations. They partner closely with Developers and Product Managers to close quality gaps before releases.

### Responsibilities
- Define and execute test plans, test cases, and exploratory testing
- Automate regression and integration tests
- Report, triage, and track defects through resolution
- Validate acceptance criteria and definition of done
- Collaborate on risk-based testing prioritization

### Goals
- Prevent defects from reaching production
- Reduce regression risk through automated coverage
- Provide clear, timely quality signals to the team

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint reviews
- Defect reports and test result summaries
- Coordination with Developers on fix verification and release readiness

### Interaction with Existing Roles
- **Developers**: Partners on testability, defect triage, and automated test strategy
- **Product Managers**: Validates acceptance criteria and confirms user flows
- **Project Managers**: Reports quality status and flags risks that may affect timelines

---

## Stakeholders

### Role Summary
Stakeholders are internal or external parties with a vested interest in project outcomes. They provide strategic direction, funding, approvals, and domain expertise, and they hold the team accountable to business objectives.

### Responsibilities
- Provide business requirements, constraints, and success criteria
- Review and approve major deliverables, scope changes, and release plans
- Champion the project within their business unit or client organization
- Escalate blockers that require executive intervention
- Offer timely feedback during reviews and demos

### Goals
- Ensure the project delivers measurable business value
- Maintain visibility into progress, risks, and decisions
- Build confidence in the team's ability to deliver

### Typical Communication
- Monthly stakeholder updates from the Project Manager
- Milestone demos and review sessions
- Ad-hoc escalations and decision requests

### Interaction with Existing Roles
- **Project Managers**: Primary communication channel for status, risks, and decision requests
- **Product Managers**: Aligns on priorities, scope trade-offs, and success metrics
- **Developers**: Participates in demos; provides domain clarifications when needed

---

## UX / Design Lead

### Role Summary
UX/Design Leads are responsible for user research, interaction design, and visual specifications that ensure the product is usable, accessible, and aligned with customer needs.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and usability testing
- Create wireframes, prototypes, and design specifications
- Define interaction patterns and visual design standards
- Collaborate with Product Managers to translate user needs into requirements
- Review implemented UIs for design fidelity and accessibility compliance

### Goals
- Deliver intuitive, accessible, and consistent user experiences
- Reduce design-to-development rework through clear specifications
- Advocate for the end user in prioritization and trade-off discussions

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews and critique sessions with Developers and Product Managers
- Usability test reports and research summaries
- Annotated prototypes and design-system documentation

### Interaction with Existing Roles
- **Developers**: Provides design specs, reviews implementations, and resolves UX ambiguities
- **Product Managers**: Co-defines user stories and acceptance criteria with a UX lens
- **Project Managers**: Surfaces design-scope risks and timeline dependencies

---

## DevOps / Platform Engineer

### Role Summary
DevOps/Platform Engineers build and maintain the infrastructure, CI/CD pipelines, and operational tooling that enable teams to deliver reliably and efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Design, implement, and maintain CI/CD pipelines and deployment automation
- Manage cloud infrastructure, environments, and access controls
- Monitor system health, performance, and reliability
- Establish security and compliance guardrails
- Support Developer productivity through tooling and platform improvements

### Goals
- Shorten the feedback loop from code commit to production
- Maximize system reliability, observability, and security posture
- Reduce operational toil and manual deployment steps

### Typical Communication
- Platform updates and runbook documentation
- Incident post-mortems and reliability reports
- Coordination with Developers on environment and pipeline issues

### Interaction with Existing Roles
- **Developers**: Enables fast, reliable delivery via automated pipelines and self-service infrastructure
- **Project Managers**: Communicates infrastructure risks, deployment schedules, and environment readiness
- **QA/Testing Engineers**: Provides stable test environments and automates test execution infrastructure
